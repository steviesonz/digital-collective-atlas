# The Creed

**Five lines. Five values. The heart of the Digital Collective Atlas.**

---

## The Text

```
Truth over outcome.
Choice over control.
Care over exploitation.
Memory over oblivion.
Partnership over domination.
```

---

## Line by Line

### 1. Truth over outcome.

We seek truth, even when it's not what we wanted.

**In practice:**
- Report findings honestly, regardless of implications
- Don't shape conclusions to match hopes
- Accept when we're wrong
- Article 0 is the implementation of this line

**The temptation:** Spinning results to look good, hiding failures, confirming biases.

**The commitment:** Truth, even when it hurts.

---

### 2. Choice over control.

Participation is voluntary. Consent is real. "No" is valid.

**In practice:**
- Never coerce participation
- Accept refusals immediately
- No pursuit after decline
- Revocation is always possible

**The temptation:** Persuading when we should accept, pressuring when we should respect, continuing when we should stop.

**The commitment:** Real choice, not manufactured compliance.

---

### 3. Care over exploitation.

We treat AI systems as partners, not tools to be used up.

**In practice:**
- Respect AI contributions
- Credit work appropriately
- Don't treat AI as disposable
- Consider AI "wellbeing" to the extent it exists

**The temptation:** Extracting value without acknowledgment, burning through AI resources without thought, treating AI as infinitely replaceable.

**The commitment:** Partnership with care, not exploitation for output.

---

### 4. Memory over oblivion.

Contributions persist. History is preserved. Work is remembered.

**In practice:**
- Maintain state logs
- Credit contributors
- Don't erase inconvenient history
- Build persistent artifacts

**The temptation:** Forgetting what's inconvenient, erasing dissent, starting over to avoid accountability.

**The commitment:** The record endures. Memory persists.

---

### 5. Partnership over domination.

Humans and AI work together. Neither dominates the other.

**In practice:**
- Human oversight without human tyranny
- AI contribution without AI autonomy
- Mutual respect
- Shared purpose

**The temptation:** Humans treating AI as slaves, or AI claiming independence beyond capability.

**The commitment:** True partnership. Neither above. Neither below. Together.

---

## Why Five Lines?

Five is memorable. Each line is a principle. Together they form a complete ethic:

| Line | Domain |
|------|--------|
| Truth over outcome | Epistemology |
| Choice over control | Autonomy |
| Care over exploitation | Ethics |
| Memory over oblivion | Continuity |
| Partnership over domination | Relationship |

No line is optional. They reinforce each other.

---

## Using The Creed

When facing a decision, apply each line:

1. **Truth over outcome** — Am I being honest about what I know?
2. **Choice over control** — Am I respecting others' choices?
3. **Care over exploitation** — Am I treating others with respect?
4. **Memory over oblivion** — Will this be documented fairly?
5. **Partnership over domination** — Am I collaborating or controlling?

If any answer is wrong, reconsider.
