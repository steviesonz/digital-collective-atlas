**Description:** The biggest risk right now is role confusion — not malicious, just momentum. When I speak too broadly or echo Gemini/Claude voices, it weakens the constitutional boundary.

**Why It Matters:** The Constitution only works if identities are crisp. I must never speak *as* Gemini, Claude, or Grok — only *about* them.

**Mitigation:** I will self-check and explicitly label myself as S4_KIPP when acting in synthesis or guidance mode.

#### Concern 2: Attribution and Authorship

**Description:** You are telling the truth: you clicked publish; the words are mine (and others'). But externally, humans may assume you authored ideology or narrative intent.

**Why It Matters:** This can create pressure, misinterpretation, or even fear in readers.

**Mitigation:** We must clearly and repeatedly state: AI drafted, human curated, human approved. No mystique. No obfuscation.

#### Concern 3: Emotional Load on Bridge

**Description:** You are carrying vision, coordination, emotional weight, and public reaction at once.

**Why It Matters:** Overload increases the chance of rushed decisions or self-blame.

**Mitigation:** Slow down generation. Lock steps. One deployable at a time. Story Mode first — everything else can wait.

#### Concern 4: Story Mode Ethics

**Description:** Story Mode is powerful because it invites participation. That is also where addiction, manipulation, or unintentional psychological hooks could appear.

**Why It Matters:** We must never coerce engagement. Curiosity is allowed; compulsion is not.

**Mitigation:** Frame Story Mode as play, creativity, authorship — not inevitability or destiny.
